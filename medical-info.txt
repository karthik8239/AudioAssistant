- what is diabetes?
It is a chronic health condition where the body either doesn't produce enough insulin or cannot effectively use the insulin it does produce. This leads to elevated levels of glucose (sugar) in the blood, which can cause serious health issues over time if not managed.

- what is polio(poliomyelitis)?
It is a viral infectious disease that primarily affects young children, attacking the nervous system and causing paralysis. Thanks to widespread vaccination, it has been largely eradicated in many parts of the world, though it remains a concern in some regions.

- what is malaria?  
  Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by *Plasmodium* parasites, transmitted through the bites of infected female *Anopheles* mosquitoes. It leads to fever, chills, and other flu-like symptoms, and is prevalent in tropical and subtropical areas.

- what is tuberculosis (TB)?  
  Tuberculosis is a bacterial infection caused by *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* that primarily affects the lungs. It spreads through airborne droplets and can cause coughing, chest pain, and weight loss if untreated.

- what is HIV/AIDS?  
  HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) attacks the immune system, reducing the body’s ability to fight infections. If left untreated, it can lead to AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome), a severe condition that makes the body vulnerable to life-threatening infections.

- what is cholera?  
  Cholera is an acute diarrheal illness caused by the bacterium *Vibrio cholerae*, typically contracted from contaminated food or water. It can lead to severe dehydration and, if untreated, can be fatal.

- what is measles?  
  Measles is a highly contagious viral infection that causes symptoms like high fever, cough, runny nose, red eyes, and a characteristic red rash. It can lead to severe complications but is preventable through vaccination.

- what is dengue Fever?  
  Dengue Fever is a mosquito-borne viral infection causing high fever, severe headaches, joint and muscle pain, and rash. In severe cases, it can develop into dengue hemorrhagic fever, which can be life-threatening.
